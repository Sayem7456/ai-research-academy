<Callout type="info">Limits describe the behavior of functions as inputs approach a value — the foundational concept upon which all of calculus is built.</Callout>

## What Problem Does It Solve?

What happens to a function as its input gets closer and closer to some value? Limits give us a rigorous way to talk about "approaching" without actually reaching a point — essential for defining derivatives, continuity, and convergence.

## Intuition

Consider $\frac{\sin(x)}{x}$. At $x = 0$ this is undefined ($0/0$). But as $x$ gets closer to $0$, the ratio gets closer to $1$:

$$
\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin(x)}{x} = 1
$$

## Formal Definition

$$
\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L
$$

means: for every $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $\delta > 0$ such that $0 < |x - a| < \delta \implies |f(x) - L| < \epsilon$.

## Limit Laws

If $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$ and $\lim_{x \to a} g(x) = M$:

| Law | Formula |
|-----|---------|
| Sum | $\lim [f(x) + g(x)] = L + M$ |
| Product | $\lim [f(x) \cdot g(x)] = L \cdot M$ |
| Quotient | $\lim \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{L}{M}$ (if $M \neq 0$) |
| Power | $\lim [f(x)]^n = L^n$ |

## Squeeze Theorem

If $g(x) \leq f(x) \leq h(x)$ near $a$, and $\lim_{x \to a} g(x) = \lim_{x \to a} h(x) = L$, then:

$$
\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L
$$

## Limits at Infinity

$$
\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{x} = 0, \quad \lim_{x \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{1}{x}\right)^x = e
$$

These limits describe the long-term behavior of functions — crucial for understanding convergence of learning algorithms.

<Callout type="tip">In machine learning, limits appear everywhere: the limit of gradient descent as iterations → ∞, the behavior of loss functions, and the definition of derivatives themselves.</Callout>

```python
import numpy as np

# Numerical approximation of lim(x→0) sin(x)/x
for x in [0.1, 0.01, 0.001, 0.0001]:
    print(f"x = {x}: sin(x)/x = {np.sin(x)/x:.8f}")
# All approach 1.0

# lim(x→∞) (1 + 1/x)^x → e
for x in [10, 100, 1000, 10000]:
    print(f"x = {x}: (1+1/x)^x = {(1 + 1/x)**x:.6f}")
# Approaches e ≈ 2.718282
```

## Key Takeaway

Limits are the foundation of calculus. They rigorously define "approaching" a value and enable the formal definitions of derivatives and integrals. In AI, understanding limits helps reason about convergence of optimization algorithms and the behavior of functions near critical points.
